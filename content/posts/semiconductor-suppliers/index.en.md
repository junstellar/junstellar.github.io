Trade stocks long enough and you meet these names: not Samsung, not hynix, yet they rise when hynix posts strong earnings and swing whenever HBM makes headlines. In Korea they're called **"so-bu-jang."** They're the names I flagged as the third floodgate of the money river in [part 6](/en/p/ai-money-flow/). Today: what this supplier ecosystem actually is, and why it moves as one with hynix.

## So-bu-jang = materials, components, equipment

"So-bu-jang" is an acronym for **so**jae (materials), **bu**pum (components), and **jang**bi (equipment). Not the companies that make and sell chips (Samsung, hynix), but **the companies that supply them the materials, parts, and machines to make chips.** A construction analogy makes it click.

![Diagram of the three supplier types — materials, components, equipment — as a construction analogy](supplier-types.png)

- **Materials**: consumables used in processing — photoresist, specialty gases, cleaning solutions, precursors. **Consumed every time the fab runs.**
- **Components**: parts that go into equipment or chips — test sockets that check chips, substrates that carry them. **More needed as output rises.**
- **Equipment**: the machines that make chips — TC bonders that stack and bond HBM chips, etch/deposition tools, inspection tools. **Ordered in bulk when capacity is expanded.**

If hynix is the building owner, the moment it decides to expand, the cement, window, and crane firms all get busy at once.

## Why they move with hynix — the order chain

The key is the **order in the trickle-down.** Remember the money river from part 6? When AI demand grows, the stream passes downstream in steps.

![Diagram of why suppliers move with hynix — the order chain](supplier-chain.png)

Rising AI server demand → hynix and Samsung decide to expand HBM output → **bulk equipment orders** → fabs run flat-out, **continuously consuming materials and components.** So a single headline — "hynix expands HBM capacity" — becomes an earnings preview for the whole supplier chain. That's why suppliers react so sensitively to hynix's stock.

## Where the money lands — why these companies pop

Concrete examples make it fast. (Below is illustration of the mechanism, not stock recommendations.)

- **Equipment — TC bonders**: part 1 showed HBM stacks DRAM vertically. The core machine that precisely stacks and bonds those chips is the TC bonder. The more HBM, and the higher the stacks, the more sophisticated the bonders needed. A Korean maker holds a ~70% global share here, hence the "flagship HBM equipment" label.
- **Components — test sockets, substrates**: sockets that connect chips to test gear, substrates that carry chips — **consumed in proportion to output.** A play tied to production itself, not just to expansion.
- **Materials — specialty gases, precursors, cleaning solutions**: consumables that keep selling as long as fabs run. If equipment sells "once, big," materials sell "steadily" — a different earnings-volatility profile.

## What investors should watch

- **Suppliers are high-beta leverage**: if hynix is the amplifier (part 3), suppliers are leverage bolted onto that amplifier. They rise more in a good cycle and fall more when it turns — because earnings collapse when orders stop.
- **Equipment vs materials differ in character**: equipment names explode during the expansion cycle (order concentration) but face a gap once orders end. Materials names track fab utilization and are steadier. Your choice depends on what you want.
- **Customer-concentration risk**: many suppliers depend on a few large customers (hynix, Samsung) for revenue. One customer's ordering decision drives earnings, so watch the majors' results and capex plans too.
- **Doubt the "HBM stock" label**: among names lumped into the theme, many have negligible actual HBM revenue. What matters is how much of that company's revenue is really HBM/AI.

## Recap

- Suppliers = **materials, components, equipment**. They don't sell chips directly, but they're indispensable to making them.
- The **order chain** — AI demand → HBM expansion → equipment orders → material/component consumption — is why they move as one with hynix.
- Suppliers are the cycle's **leverage** (bigger up, bigger down), and equipment names (expansion-linked) differ from materials names (utilization-linked). Check **actual HBM revenue share**, not the theme label.
