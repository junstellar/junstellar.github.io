I decided to write about AI on this blog. But I spent a long time figuring out what the very first post should be. So I just went with the thing that fills my head the most these days — how I came to see AI the way I do.

## At first, I really thought it was useless

When GPT first came out, it was impressive, but the moment I actually put it to work, it was pretty rough. It talked a good game but had no substance, and the moment you went a little deeper, it spouted nonsense. "Just a neat toy," I thought, and moved on. I honestly never imagined it would change my life.

But in less than a year, by my own reckoning, the world had changed. Trying out one thing after another, from GPT to Claude Code, that once-clumsy tool now does almost everything I can think of. It's so fast it's hard to keep up. A tool I called "useless" not long ago — now I can't picture working without it.

## The 'how' is solved, but now 'what' is the homework

Here's the big thing I felt.

Before, "how do I even do this?" was always the wall. I'd stop because I didn't know the method, because my hands couldn't keep up. But now AI solves the *how*. Someone with no way with words and no writing talent — me — now feels like he can write almost as well as he codes for a living.

And once that wall disappeared, a completely different question was left behind.

**"So then — what should I actually do?"**

This one is harder. The more I'm able to do, the more lost I get about what I *should* do. There's also a strange feeling — the fact that I can now do this means everyone else can too. In an age where anyone holds the same tools, what am I supposed to do?

Honestly, I'll admit it: I was always someone with lots of things he wanted to do but who never actually did any of them. I made excuses like "I couldn't because I didn't know how," but the real problem was never the how — it was the *what*, and the will to actually start.

## So I decided to fumble through it — together

But as I used AI, that wall got lower. Things I couldn't even begin because they felt overwhelming, I can now just take a swing at. And the first *what* I found is exactly this — sharing my AI story.

The thing is, the clean, well-organized "right answers" from the experts already flood the world. So I want to go the other way. Rather than what went well, I'll put out the stuff I fumbled, the things that failed, the ones that blew up spectacularly — just as they are. There will be more failures than successes anyway, and honestly, that part is more fun.

I just want to share — as an ordinary person, at an ordinary person's eye level — the things I bumped into and learned. I may not have an expert's depth, but I believe the view of someone fumbling from the same starting line is genuinely useful. If I can laugh through the trial and error together with people who, like me, thought "this is cool, but how do I even use it?" — that's enough.

AI solved the *how*. Now, what to share with that tool — it starts right here.

Slowly, but surely — to the Moon. 🚀
